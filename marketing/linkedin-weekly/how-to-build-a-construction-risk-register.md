## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach how-to-build-a-construction-risk-register-listicle.png)

Almost every project that fails on cost or programme traces back to a risk that was either never identified or never properly managed.

A risk register is the document that forces the team to think ahead instead of firefighting. But only if it is built properly.

Here is what every row in a construction risk register should contain:

→ A clear cause-and-effect description (if, then)
→ Likelihood x impact score, 1 to 25
→ A named owner, not "the team"
→ A response: avoid, reduce, transfer or accept
→ Mitigation actions with real dates
→ A residual score that proves the mitigation works

"Bad weather" is not a risk. "If sustained rain hits the groundworks phase, then the dig floods and the slab pour slips" is a risk you can score, own and manage.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach how-to-build-a-construction-risk-register-hook.png)

A blank risk register is one of the most daunting documents on a project.

The slow part is the first draft: thinking of every plausible risk, writing each in proper cause-and-effect form, categorising it, and suggesting sensible mitigations.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Describe the project to AI: type, location, contract, programme, constraints
2. Ask for a starter register across the standard risk categories
3. Challenge it: delete what does not apply, add the site-specific risks only you know, adjust the scores
4. Maintain it each review cycle

Half a day of staring at an empty spreadsheet becomes twenty minutes. The AI brings the breadth and structure, you bring the judgement.
