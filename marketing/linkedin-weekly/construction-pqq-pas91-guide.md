## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach construction-pqq-pas91-guide-listicle.png)

Most PQQs are not lost on capability. They are lost on admin.

Here are the five things that quietly fail a pre-qualification questionnaire:

→ An insurance certificate that expires days after the deadline
→ A health and safety policy nobody signed or dated
→ A RIDDOR incident you hoped they would not check
→ Case studies from the wrong sector, however big the value
→ Three paragraphs of preamble in a 200-word field

The third one is worth dwelling on. Buyers do not expect a groundworks contractor to have a zero-incident history. They expect evidence that you learned something. Disclose the incident, describe what changed, move on.

Concealing it turns a survivable safety record into a disqualifying integrity failure, and the HSE record is public anyway.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach construction-pqq-pas91-guide-hook.png)

PAS 91 was withdrawn by BSI in 2022. Plenty of tenders still ask for it.

If a buyer asks you for "PAS 91 compliance" in 2026, one of three things is true: they are using an old template, they mean "a standard PQQ" generically, or they never updated. None of it is a problem for you, because the questions barely changed.

What did change is the fix. Build UK's Common Assessment Standard is assessed once, and the certificate is accepted by every buyer who recognises it. No more rewriting the same eight answers for every opportunity.

If you tender more than about four times a year, that is the single biggest time saving available to you in pre-qualification. It pays for itself in roughly three tenders.

If you tender less often, build a reusable answer library instead. Two days of work up front turns the next Friday-deadline-Tuesday PQQ into a mapping exercise rather than a weekend.

Both routes, plus the AI workflow that quality-checks the pack before you submit: https://buildcopilot.ai/guides/construction-pqq-pas91-guide
