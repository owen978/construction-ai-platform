## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach cdm-construction-phase-plan-guide-listicle.png)

A lot of contractors think the construction phase plan is only for the big jobs.

It is not. Under CDM 2015 every construction project needs one, from a £50m hospital down to a two-week extension. What changes is the depth, not whether you need it.

Here is what a proportionate construction phase plan actually has to cover:

→ Project details and programme
→ How the work will be managed for health and safety
→ Site rules: PPE, permits, sign-in, restricted areas
→ Arrangements for the significant risks that this job carries
→ Welfare, access, and emergency arrangements
→ Reviewed and updated at each stage of the build

The biggest mistake is copying the last job's plan and listing forty generic hazards. A good plan names the real risks of this site and how you will control them, and nothing more.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach cdm-construction-phase-plan-guide-hook.png)

No construction phase plan, no start on site. That is the rule under CDM 2015, on every project, however small.

The problem is that writing a good one from a blank page eats the best part of a day, and most small contractors have no dedicated health and safety team to do it.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Gather the pre-construction info, the programme, and the significant risks you already know
2. Prompt AI to draft a plan structured to CDM 2015 and HSE L153
3. Review hard: cut anything that does not apply to this site, add what only you know
4. Attach your site layout and the trade RAMS

A day-long job becomes well under an hour. You spend your time on the judgement, not the formatting.
