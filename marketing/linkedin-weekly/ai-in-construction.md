## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach ai-in-construction-listicle.png)

When people hear "AI in construction" they picture robots laying bricks.

That is not where the money is. Not yet, anyway.

Construction runs on documents. Specs, contracts, RAMS, reports, tenders. That is exactly what AI is good at right now, and the time savings are not small:

→ Method statements and RAMS: 60-80% faster
→ Daily reports from rough notes: around 80%
→ Cost plans and BoQ review: 50-70%
→ Tender analysis and comparison: 60-75%
→ Contract clause review: 50-70%
→ Meeting minutes and actions: around 75%

Notice the pattern. AI does the reading, drafting and formatting. You do the judgement, the pricing and the sign-off.

The drudge work goes. The expert work stays. That is the whole point.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach ai-in-construction-hook.png)

A site manager I know spent 40 minutes editing last year's method statement for a precast staircase lift.

Same task with AI: a short brief covering the plant, the hazards and BS 7121, and a structured first draft comes back in under a minute. Scope, sequence, exclusion zone, banksman roles, emergency arrangements.

He then spent 10 minutes correcting the crane position, adding the lift plan reference and confirming the competent person names.

40 minutes became 10. And the draft was more thorough, because AI does not skip standard sections when it is tired at 5pm.

Two things worth saying plainly:

1. You still sign it off. Under CDM 2015 the duty does not transfer to a tool.
2. You can test all of this on a free tier before spending a penny.

Start with one task you do often. Prove it. Then expand.
