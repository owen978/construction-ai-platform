## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach daily-report-listicle.png)

Most site managers write the daily report at the end of a long day, when they are tired and want to go home.

That is exactly when records get thin. And thin records cost you in disputes.

Here is what a daily site report should actually capture, every single day:

→ Weather and any time lost to it
→ Labour, plant and deliveries
→ Work completed, by location
→ Delays and their specific causes
→ Verbal instructions received
→ Date-stamped photos

One ordinary day often contains three pieces of claim evidence: a weather delay, a late delivery, and a verbal instruction that becomes a variation.

Your daily report is not box-ticking. It is the contemporaneous record that wins or loses claims months later.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach daily-report-hook.png)

A construction daily report written from memory three days later is almost worthless as evidence.

But writing a detailed one at 5pm after a 10-hour day is the last thing anyone wants to do.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Bullet-point notes on your phone during the day
2. Paste them into AI with a structured prompt
3. Get a full, professional daily report back
4. Review, add photos, submit

30 minutes becomes 5. And the records stay detailed even on the hard days.
