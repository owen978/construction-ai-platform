## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach construction-meeting-minutes-guide-listicle.png)

A page of minutes that says "the team discussed drainage" is worthless.

"Action: groundworks to confirm plot 16 connection by Fri 26 Jun" is gold.

The difference is everything in construction meeting minutes. Here is what the good ones always do:

→ Record decisions, not discussion
→ Give every action a named owner
→ Put a due date on every action
→ Number actions and carry them forward
→ Confirm verbal instructions in writing
→ Circulate within 24 hours

One ordinary progress meeting often holds a closed action, a slipping programme, and a verbal instruction that becomes a variation. Your minutes are the contemporaneous record that decides the claim months later.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach construction-meeting-minutes-guide-hook.png)

Writing up site meeting minutes an hour later, from scrappy notes, when the detail has already faded.

That is when actions get lost and verbal instructions go unrecorded.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Bullet-point notes during the meeting (or a transcript)
2. Paste them into AI with a structured prompt
3. Get full minutes back, with an action tracker
4. Review the decisions, fix owners and dates, circulate

An hour becomes 5 minutes. And the actions stay sharp because you are not rebuilding them from memory days later.

One rule: never let AI decide the decisions. The chair always owns the final record.
