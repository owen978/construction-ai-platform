## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach ai-tender-analysis-guide-listicle.png)

The cheapest tender return is often the most expensive bid once you read the small print.

The number that sinks a project is rarely on the summary page. It is a one-line exclusion three appendices deep: "assumes clean site", "excludes works below existing ground level".

Here is how AI helps you catch it before you appoint:

→ Normalise every bid onto a like-for-like basis
→ Extract every exclusion, qualification and assumption
→ Flag where a bid deviates from the requirements
→ Draft a side-by-side comparison table
→ Draft a first-pass scoring matrix
→ Humans still value the risk and make the call

AI reads three returns in minutes and surfaces the exclusions that change the answer. You value them and decide.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach ai-tender-analysis-guide-hook.png)

Three groundworks bids. Headline prices: £842k, £791k, £868k.

On the summary page, the £791k bid wins.

Then you normalise: it excluded muck away, priced out contaminated ground, and left scaffolding to the retaining wall unpriced. Add those back and it is no longer the cheapest, and its programme was three weeks shorter than everyone else's.

Reading three full returns to find that used to take a day or two.

The workflow now:

1. Feed the returns to AI with a structured prompt
2. Get every bid normalised to the same scope
3. Get every exclusion flagged with a page reference
4. Check the numbers against source, then decide

Two days becomes a few hours. And the decision still belongs to a qualified human.

Step-by-step here: https://buildcopilot.ai/guides/ai-tender-analysis-guide
