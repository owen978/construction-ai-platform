## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach construction-cost-plan-guide-listicle.png)

A budget figure on its own is almost useless.

"The scheme is 2.4 million" tells you nothing about whether the number is right, where the risk sits, or what to cut if the tender comes back high.

A proper cost plan turns that single number into something you can actually control. Built on the NRM1 elements, it should cover:

→ Elemental works cost (substructure to external works)
→ Preliminaries and overheads and profit
→ Risk allowances, split by type and reducing each stage
→ Inflation, to both tender and completion
→ A clear list of assumptions and exclusions

On the worked example in the guide, over 770,000 sat on top of a works cost of under 2 million once prelims, OH&P, risk and inflation were added.

Anyone estimating a building on works cost alone will be badly wrong.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach construction-cost-plan-guide-hook.png)

The slow part of cost planning is not the judgement. It is the setup.

Building the elemental structure, drafting the assumptions and exclusions, writing the risk narrative that explains the plan to the client. That is the afternoon that disappears before you have priced a single line.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Feed the AI the project parameters (type, GIA, storeys, location, quality)
2. Ask for a first-pass NRM1 elemental cost plan with rates flagged for you to confirm
3. Have it draft the assumptions, exclusions and risk narrative
4. Apply your real rates, adjust the risk to the stage, and check nothing was invented

A blank spreadsheet becomes a structured first draft in minutes. The judgement stays with the surveyor.
