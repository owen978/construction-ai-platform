## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach construction-cost-plan-guide-listicle.png)

"It's about 2.6 million" is where a lot of construction budgets go wrong.

On a recent worked example, the building works were only 64 percent of the final cost estimate. The rest? Preliminaries, fees, risk, and inflation. Miss those early and the number is wrong before design even starts.

A proper NRM1 cost plan builds it up properly:

→ Build up by NRM1 elements, not one lump sum
→ Name and price every risk (not a vague 5% contingency)
→ Add inflation to the construction midpoint
→ Prelims are often 10 to 20% of works cost
→ Reconcile against the last cost plan every stage
→ Fees, OH&P and other development costs, in the open

A cost plan is a living document, not a one-off number. It matures from cost per m2 to measured quantities as the design develops.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach construction-cost-plan-guide-hook.png)

Assembling a preliminary cost plan is mostly structure, benchmark data, and boilerplate. The judgement is the QS's. The formatting is not.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Give AI the brief: GIFA, storeys, use class, location, spec, budget
2. Ask it to structure the cost plan by NRM1 elements
3. Add prelims, OH&P, fees, risk and inflation as explicit lines
4. Replace every benchmark rate with your firm's own data
5. Let it draft the basis-of-estimate narrative

The rule that matters: treat every AI number as a placeholder until you have replaced it with a rate you can stand behind. AI handles structure and drafting. The numbers stay yours.
