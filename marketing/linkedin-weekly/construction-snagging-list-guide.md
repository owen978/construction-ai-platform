## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach construction-snagging-list-guide-listicle.png)

A snag list with no priority column is just a wish list.

If every item looks equally urgent, the team cannot tell which snags actually block handover and which are cosmetic. So practical completion slips, and the retention sits there.

Here is what every snagging entry should actually capture:

→ Precise location, not just the room
→ The trade responsible for the fix
→ Priority: does it block handover or not
→ Date-stamped photo evidence
→ Status tracked from open to closed
→ Verification before it is signed off

Get those six right and you reach practical completion faster, release the first half of the retention sooner, and shorten the defects liability period.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach construction-snagging-list-guide-hook.png)

Finding the snags is the easy part. Writing them up is what eats your evening.

You walk the plot, scribble "kitchen, cracked tile, splashback" and ten more lines, then lose an hour turning it into a tidy schedule and emailing the right subbie.

Here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Rough notes or voice memos on site
2. Paste them into AI with a structured prompt
3. Get a full snag schedule back: refs, trades, priorities, status
4. Review, grade the priorities, add photos, issue

An hour of admin becomes a few minutes. And because it is no longer a chore, it actually gets done the same day while the inspection is fresh.
