## Post 1 — Value / listicle (attach rams-listicle.png)

Most RAMS get rejected for the same reason. They could have been written for any site.

A RAMS is two documents in one. The risk assessment says what could go wrong. The method statement says how you will stop it. Principal contractors look for the same sections every time:

→ Project and scope details
→ Risk assessment with scoring, before and after controls
→ Step-by-step safe method
→ Required PPE and competence
→ Emergency arrangements
→ Sign-off and briefing record

The last one is the one people forget. The document can be perfect, but with no proof the team was briefed, it fails.

And "general building works" is not a scope. "Installation of stud partitions to second floor, grid lines A to D" is.

## Post 2 — Hook / time-saver (attach rams-hook.png)

Writing a RAMS from a blank page takes a competent person two to four hours.

Most of that is structure and wording, not safety thinking.

So here is the workflow we recommend:

1. Give the AI the task, the working environment, the height, the substances and the equipment
2. Let it draft the scope, the hazard list with scoring, the controls and the method sequence
3. Review hard against the actual site: add what only you know, cut what does not apply, correct the scoring
4. Add the human-only parts: named operatives, competence records, site emergency arrangements
5. Brief the team and record it

Two to four hours becomes twenty to thirty minutes.

Worth saying plainly: a RAMS generated and signed off without review is dangerous. A RAMS generated and then properly reviewed by a competent person is just as valid as one typed from scratch, and far quicker to produce.

The AI replaces the blank page, not your judgement.

## Post 3 — Short / group version (attach rams-hook.png)

Quick one for the site managers and subcontractors here.

RAMS stands for Risk Assessment and Method Statement. The term itself is not in law, but everything in it is. The Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 require a suitable and sufficient risk assessment, and CDM 2015 requires the work to be planned and carried out safely.

We put together a free guide and template covering every section a principal contractor looks for, a worked example for a mobile tower scaffold, and how to draft one in minutes with AI.
